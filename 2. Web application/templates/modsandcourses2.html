Breaking down "modules mentioned" and identifying types of CS confessions

Up to now, we have been talking down about mentions of a module in a confession.
However, there are two different cases where a student will mention a module:
1) Asking for more information about a module eg. "Has anyone taken CS2030?"
2) Sharing his/her experience about a module eg. "CS2030 midterm was very hard"

Moreover, there are different levels of modules: the students' first CS module (CS1010, CS1010S, CS1010E) and higher-level CS modules.

Now, let's summarize the previous breakdown of "modules mentioned" and consequently try to identify 4 different groups of CS students on NUSWhispers.
We want to do this because segmenting students into groups allows us to discover interesting insights such as
how many students want to enter CS and how much each group is growing each year.

While the groups are largely mutually exclusive, it seems that they are related in some way.
Let's plot the journey of a CS Student on NUSWhispers to make the relationship clear. Let's name the student Jack.

Step 1: When deciding to enter CS, Jack decides to ask about his first CS module CS1010. At this point, he belongs to group 1.
Step 2: During the course, Jack decides to share his/her experience about the module. At this point, he belongs to group 3.
Step 3: After completing the first CS module, Jack asks about higher-level CS modules, therefore belonging to group 2.
Step 4: During the higher-level module, Jack shares about his experience, therefore belonging to group 4.
Step 5a: After the course, Jack proceeds to take other modules, therefore repeating steps 3 and 4.
Step 5b: After Jack has fulfilled his module requirements, he graduates. However, sometimes he sees interesting things on NUSWhispers
and decides to share about his experience in CS1010 or higher-level CS modules. Thus, he is classified under group 3 or group 4 again.

This journey map illustrates that the groups are related and a student belonging to one group can convert to other groups as
the student becomes more advanced and takes higher-level modules. However, this also alerts a problem: it is possible for one student
to convert to another group within the same year. For instance, a student can ask about a first CS module and then share about it
when he is taking the course, therefore belonging to group 1 and group 3 in the same year.

This means that he will be double counted under 2 groups in the same year, which makes the calculation inaccurate.
However, given that each confession is anonymous, we can't trace back confessions to identify which students made the confessions.
As such, we will make a big assumption that one student only makes one confession (and not multiple confessions) in one year.
Following this, we can plot the growth of each group across years:

We can observe that group 1 (students that want to enter CS) is growing exponentially, showing that more and more students have an interest to enter CS.
Group 4 (sharing about higher level CS mods) also appears to growing at a rapid rate. This can be due to multiple reasons:

1) students are becoming more active on NUSWhispers in general and are more willing to share their experiences with higher level CS mods,
2) students are having more frustrations with higher level CS mods (eg. CS2030) resulting in more students turning to NUSWhispers to voice their displeasure,
3) more students are converting from other groups to group 4 as they transit from taking their first CS module to higher level CS modules.

This analysis has been an effort to show not only the most popular modules mentioned, but going deeper into understanding
students' motivations in mentioning the modules and how that factors into the grander scheme of a student's university journey.
