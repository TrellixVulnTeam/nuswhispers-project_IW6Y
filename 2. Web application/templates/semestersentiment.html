Semester sentiment

Having been through 4 years of university, I know how hard it can get during school semesters.
Therefore, I would like to investigate how students feel during schooling vs non-schooling semesters
and how their sentiments fluctuate across the weeks during a schooling semester.

How students feel during schooling semesters vs non-schooling semester

As expected, off-sem has the highest score, meaning that people feel the most positive during a non-schooling semester.
On the other hand, the semester where people feel the least positive is sem 1.
Having talked to one of my NUS friends, she thinks that sem 1 is the semester where freshies are starting out
and people are coming back from exchange. Thus, perhaps, most people are still not used to the workload
and the overall sentiment is lower.

How students feel across the weeks during schooling semesters

We can now look in detail into students' sentiments across the weeks.

Overall, as we mentioned above, students have more positive sentiments during sem 2 compared to sem 1.
In addition, we see a downward trend for both semesters; students generally get less positive as the weeks pass.

Across the weeks, we observe a few peaks and troughs. Let's try and address them one by one.
The first peak is around W3/W4. It is likely because students have finalized their modules and no longer have to worry.
Moreover, at this point, they have probably gotten accustomed to their classes and made friends.

However, sentiment decreases from this point as students' workload increases. It is likely that there are troughs in W6 and W7 of sem 1 due to midterms.
Comparatively, W6 and W7 of Sem 2 have more positive sentiments, perhaps because students are more used to doing midterms.
What happens after W7 is interesting: Sem 2 experiences a trough in W8 and a peak in W9. Both semesters also experience a peak in W12.
This is surprising to me, as I thought W12 and W13 are project weeks and students will be more stressed during this period.

Thereafter, we see a decrease in sentiment as exams approach, as expected. However, sentiment for sem 2 is higher
than that for sem 1 during exam period. This is likely because there is a 4 months break
after sem 2 compared to 1 month break for sem 1.
