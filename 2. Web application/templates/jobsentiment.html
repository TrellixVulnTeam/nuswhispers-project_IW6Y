Job Sentiment

Given the US-China trade war and many articles reporting how difficult it is to find jobs,
I thought it would be interesting to find out how people feel about finding jobs over the years.

How people feel about jobs in general over the years

To determine whether a confession is positive or negative,
we can assign scores to characters/words/sentences in the confession.
For instance, a smiley face ":)" or the word "good" will have a higher score.

Therefore, higher scores mean the confessions are more positive,
while lower scores mean the confessions are more negative.

From the graph, we can observe a significant dip in job sentiment in 2018.
We can look at the confessions in 2018 to understand why.

It appears that there are many negative confessions in 2018.
However, we see that these confessions are related to jobs in general, but not specifically finding jobs.

How people feel about finding jobs over the years

To identify sentiment for "finding jobs", I first filtered the dataset by identifying confessions that contain "find job" or secure employment."
I decided to exclude year 2018 from the analysis since its sample size is too small (n < 30).
Because the tool I used for sentiment analysis has a tendency to misclassify negative sentiments as positive,
I decided to just focus on the negative sentiments, which is primarily what we are interested in.

We can clearly see that the negative "finding job" sentiments become more negative across the years.
Like what we hypothesized, 2019 has the most negative sentiment because of the poor job market.
